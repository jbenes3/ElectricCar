Summary & Insights

  • EV adoption is concentrated in specific areas:
    • EV usage is not evenly distributed across the state.
    • King County alone accounts for 50.63% of all EVs in Washington.
    • This may be due to a combination of factors like more charging stations, higher population density, stronger policies, and greater public interest in EVs.
  • Electric range has significantly improved over time:
    • Since 2018, new EV models offer much longer electric ranges.
    • This increase in range helps explain the growing popularity of EVs.
    • The D3 bar chart clearly shows this improvement in technology year by year.
  • Tesla dominates EV adoption across the state:
    • Models like the Tesla Model 3 and Model Y appear in counties all across Washington.
    • The Treemap diagram maps out the widespread presence of Tesla vehicles.
    • This highlights Tesla’s strong brand influence and broad appeal.
  • Policy and planning implications:
    • The visualizations identify areas that could benefit from additional EV infrastructure or incentives.
    • Strategies from successful areas like King County could be adapted for other regions.
  • Future directions:
    • Analyzing how EV distribution changes over time could provide better insights.
    • Comparing Washington’s trends with other states could show how it fits into the national picture.
